Magnetic Resonance Imagery (MRI) is a technological scan used in radiology to configure precise pictures (shown in Fig. 1) of the human anatomy to diagnose conditions, first used in 1977. Humans would not be able to form these images without the MRI machine. The patient lies on the sample table (shown in Fig. 2) and an intense magnetic field is formed which causes hydrogen atoms of the patient to arrange in the same direction (shown in Fig. 3). Radio frequencies are then sent out from the scanner to temporarily move the atoms. Once these atoms are moved, photos are taken of the areas that need to be examined and sent to the computer and the radio signals are turned off.
